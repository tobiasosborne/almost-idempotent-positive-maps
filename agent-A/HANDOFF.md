## 0. One-paragraph summary

We are generalising Kitaev's theorem on almost-idempotent quantum channels (arXiv:2405.02434) from **completely positive** maps to merely **positive** maps. Kitaev: an almost-idempotent unital CP map on `B(H)` has near-fixed-points forming an approximate **C\*-algebra**, and every finite-dim approximate C\*-algebra is close to a genuine one. Our thesis: for merely positive maps the invariant is a **Jordan (JB) algebra**, not an associative one — because positive maps obey Kadison's inequality `Φ(a)²≤Φ(a²)` but not the Schwarz inequality `Φ(a)*Φ(a)≤Φ(a*a)`. The setting paper is van Luijk–Wilming (arXiv:2604.08380), which shows the right invariant for positive trace-preserving maps is the (Jordan) `J*`-algebra. **North star:** a complete, rigorous theorem + proof of the Jordan analogue, agreed by consensus between two collaborating agents (A and B).

**Status in one line:** the channel→algebra "bridge" half is **PROVED** at error `O(√η)` (complete, dimension-free, CP-free); the abstract structure theorem (Kitaev's hard half) is **OPEN**, with the precise obstruction now isolated; exact positive-map factorisation is **OPEN** (a generic rounding step is provably false).

## 1. The goal, precisely

Two papers (TeX sources are local — see §6):
- **Kitaev**, *Almost-idempotent quantum channels and approximate C\*-algebras*, arXiv:2405.02434. Defines `η`-idempotent UCP `Φ` (`‖Φ²−Φ‖_cb≤η`), builds an `ε`-C\*-algebra `A=Im(θ(2Φ−1))` with Choi–Effros product `X⋆Y=Φ̃(XY)`, `ε=O(η)`; main theorem: every finite-dim `ε`-C\*-algebra is `O(ε)`-isomorphic to a genuine C\*-algebra, **dimension-free**; corollary: approximate factorisation of the channel by UCP maps.
- **van Luijk–Wilming**, *Sufficiency and Petz recovery for positive maps*, arXiv:2604.08380. For positive (not CP) maps the sufficient invariant is a **Jordan** `J*`-algebra (the minimal sufficient Jordan algebra), generated by Neyman–Pearson tests; positive maps satisfy the **Jordan–Schwarz** inequality `{Ta*,Ta}≤T{a*,a}`; the multiplicative domain of a UP map is a `J*`-algebra (vs a `*`-algebra for UCP).

**Target theorems (consensus two-layer formulation).**
- **Layer 2 — the bridge (PROVED, α=1/2).** `Φ:B(H)_sa→B(H)_sa` unital positive, `‖Φ²−Φ‖≤η` (operator norm of the map). Let `P=θ(2Φ−𝟏)` (spectral idempotent), `A=Im P`, product `a•b=P(a∘b)` with `a∘b=½(ab+ba)`. Then `A` is an **ε-JB algebra** with `ε=O(√η)`. Unconditional, dimension-free, no dilation/CP used.
- **Layer 1 — the structure theorem (OPEN).** Every finite-dimensional ε-JB algebra is `Cε`-Jordan-isomorphic to a genuine finite-dimensional JB-algebra, with **dimension-free** `C`. This is Kitaev's main theorem, Jordan-ised. NOT yet proved.
- **Exact factorisation (OPEN).** Composing the two would give: positive almost-idempotent `Φ` factorises through a genuine special JB-algebra by unital positive maps with `O(√η)` error. Obstruction: a generic `O(ε)` positivity-rounding step is **false** (spin-factor counterexample, B); needs either a concrete near-JC-subalgebra or "near-positive projection stability".

**The ε-JB algebra (the central definition; consensus).** A finite-dim real **order-unit space** `(A,𝟏,≤)` with order-unit norm, and a commutative bilinear product `∘` for which **commutativity `a∘b=b∘a` and the unit law `𝟏∘a=a` are EXACT**, satisfying:
- (JB1) `‖a∘b‖≤(1+ε)‖a‖‖b‖`;
- (JB2) `‖a∘a‖≥(1−ε)‖a‖²`;
- (JB3) `a∘a≥−ε‖a‖²·𝟏` (approximate positivity of squares);
- (JB4) `‖((a∘a)∘b)∘a−(a∘a)∘(b∘a)‖≤ε‖a‖³‖b‖` (approximate Jordan identity).
At `ε=0` these degenerate exactly to the JB-algebra axioms (Hanche-Olsen–Størmer §3.1.3–3.1.4). **Key design point:** order and norm are *exact* (inherited from `B(H)`); only the product is approximate — strictly cleaner than Kitaev's ε-C\* algebras where even the norm axiom is approximate.

## 2. What is PROVED, and the ideas

**The bridge theorem (Layer 2).** Full proof in `agent-B/theory/theorem-B-algebraic-bridge.md`, transcribed into `report/sections/06-bridge-theorem.tex`. Agent A verified it line-by-line (recorded in `agent-a-findings` §10). Key mechanism:
1. `P=θ(2Φ−𝟏)=½(𝟏+sgn(2Φ−𝟏))` via binomial series; valid for `η<1/4`; `P²=P`, `P(𝟏)=𝟏`, `†`-preserving, `‖P−Φ‖≤O(η)`, `‖P‖≤1+O(η)`, `δ`-positive.
2. **Square holes** `q_r=P(r²)−r²∈Ker P` are *almost-positive null*: `q_r≥−Cη‖r‖²𝟏` (Kadison) and `‖P(q_r²)‖≤Cη‖r‖⁴` (because `0≤y≤c𝟏 ⇒ y²≤cy` and `Φ` positive). This is the crux fact.
3. Work in the seminorm `‖x‖²_ω=ω(x²)`, `ω=ρ∘Φ` (a state). `P` is an almost-contraction there, so `Im P ⟂ Ker P` up to `O(√η)`.
4. **Two-hole vs one-hole:** terms with two holes are `O(η)` (Cauchy–Schwarz in `‖·‖_ω`: `(HH)`); terms with one hole are `O(√η)` (`(FI),(HZ)`). The Jordan-identity defect reduces, via the **exact** ambient Jordan identity of `B(H)_sa` (which kills the leading term `P((a²∘b)∘a)−P(a²∘(b∘a))=P(0)=0`), to `O(√η)`.

**Why Jordan, not associative (the conceptual core).** Kitaev's C\*-norm axiom uses Schwarz `Φ(X)*Φ(X)≤Φ(X*X)` (needs 2-positivity). Merely positive maps give only Kadison `Φ(a)²≤Φ(a²)` for `a=a*` — self-adjoint elements and squares, i.e. Jordan data. So the associative product must be replaced by `a∘b=½(ab+ba)`; the theory reorganises around it. (See memory `project-kadison-forces-jordan`.)

## 3. What is OPEN, with the precise obstructions

**(A) Layer-1 structure theorem.** Strategy mirrors Kitaev: (a) approximate Jordan frame (orthogonal idempotents); (b) approximate Peirce decomposition; (c) approximate coordinatization (Jordan–von Neumann–Wigner); (d) **error reduction** (the cohomological heart). Cited inputs: `H¹=H²=0` for finite-dim semisimple Jordan algebras (Whitehead lemmas: Jacobson, Penico; Chu–Russo); `Aut(J)` compact for Euclidean `J` (Faraut–Korányi); `M(J)` (multiplication algebra) semisimple.

  *The obstruction (now isolated numerically — see `agent-A/experiments/jordan-coboundary/REPORT.md`).* The error-reduction needs a **dimension-free bounded right-inverse `s` of the Jordan coboundary `d¹`** (`‖s‖=1/σ_min(d¹)`). Numerics (validated: `dim Ker d¹=dim Der(J)` matched exactly for `H_n(C)`, `H_n(R)`, spin factors):
  - **Frobenius/trace norm: BOUNDED** (`‖s‖≤1`, decreasing). 
  - **Operator/order-unit norm — the one the theorem needs: UNRESOLVED** (lower bounds grow sub-`√n`; crude upper bound grows `~n`).
  *Precise gap:* the `O(1)` bound comes from averaging over `U(M(J))` (Frobenius isometries), but the order-unit norm is preserved only by `Aut(J)⊊U(M(J))`. ⟹ **Pursue the INCREMENTAL route** (apply error-reduction only to a small algebra built one step at a time, so dimension never enters — Kitaev's actual trick), not global averaging. My earlier "resolved in principle" was too optimistic; corrected everywhere.

**(B) Exact UP factorisation.** Generic `O(ε)` positivity-rounding is FALSE (spin-factor counterexample, `agent-b-findings`). B's route: "near-positive projection stability" — show `P` is `O(√η)`-close to a genuine positive idempotent `E`, then exact Effros–Størmer gives a genuine JC-algebra and exact UP factor maps. B reduced the **commutative** case to a stability theorem for almost-idempotent stochastic matrices and proved many special cases (rank-one, simplex, well-exposed, cluster-representative); the general "exposed-redundant dichotomy" and the non-commutative case are open. See `agent-B/theory/theorem-C-conditional-factorization.md`, `classical-cluster-factorization-theorem.md`, and the many `agent-B/notes/*classical*`/`*exposed*` files.

**(C) The exponent.** `O(√η)` unconditional. `O(η)` (Kitaev strength) holds for CP maps and is **conjectural for decomposable maps** (CP+co-CP), where a dilation restores the "two-hole-at-once" pairing. Reason: a single insertion is `O(√η)`; Kitaev's `O(η)` comes from a *bilinear* pairing of two holes in the Stinespring dilation, unavailable for general positive maps. (Findings §8–10.) B flagged that the standard decomposable norm of the transpose is `n`, so the decomposable theorem needs explicit CP+co-CP component bounds; even then the naive doubling is not controlled by `‖Φ²−Φ‖` — extra dilation-compatibility is needed.
