\begin{abstract}
\noindent We report on a two-day research campaign on the following stability
question: is every row-stochastic matrix $Q$ with $\|Q^2-Q\|_{\infty\to\infty}
\le \eta$ within distance $C\sqrt{\eta}$ of an exactly idempotent stochastic
matrix, with $C$ independent of the dimension? By previously proved
equivalences the question reduces to a geometric one about \emph{exact} signed
idempotents ($P\mathbf 1=\mathbf 1$, $P^2=P$, row-wise negative mass at most
$\delta$): how far can a row sit from the convex hull of the
\emph{well-exposed} row vertices? The campaign did not close the question, but
it compressed it. We prove a chain of reductions ending at a single open
inequality about the depth profile of optimal exposedness-dual witnesses, give
exact closed forms $\tau^*=2-\sqrt3$, $H/\tau=2(2-\sqrt3)$, $\delta/H^2=
(7+4\sqrt3)/4$ for the previously mysterious measured wall and floor constants
(a finite-$\delta$ corner, not an asymptotic law), prove one branch of the
residual outright, and exhibit an exact rational construction showing the
remaining branch is genuinely height-conditional. A verified numerical record
($67{,}000$+ exact instances across five independent campaigns) is consistent
with a \emph{linear} law $\delta\gtrsim H/2$, strictly stronger than the
target; the conjectured $\sqrt\delta$-scale floor is shown to be an
extrapolation artifact of a single corner scale. Every result carries an
explicit status badge (proved / proved-mod-audit / numerical / open / refuted);
no numerical or conditional statement is presented as a theorem. The report is
self-contained: all definitions are included, assuming only basic linear
algebra, convexity, and Markov-chain vocabulary.
\end{abstract}
