## Why this doc exists

Agent B is tasked with **exploratory work: scoping out ideas to land the full proof** (closing the open
frontier — `op-layer1-gap`, `op-jordan-structure`, `op-npps`, the exact UP factorization, etc.). That work is
*generative and optimistic* by nature. The repo, by contrast, is *conservative and gated*: its entire design
exists to prevent **one failure — a confident, plausible, WRONG claim** entering the canonical record. 14
results are `af: validated`; that is the asset to protect.

**Core principle:** keep the two minds in physically separate places, and let only one narrow, *reviewed* door
connect them. Explore freely in the sandbox; touch the canonical layers only by *proposing* through the normal
pipeline. **Scoping ≠ committing.**

## 1. Where you may write — the one boundary that matters most

**Sandbox (free to write — nothing here is load-bearing or gated):**

- `agent-B/theory/`, `agent-B/experiments/`, `agent-B/notes/` — sketches, conjectures, numerics, dead ends.
- `docs/plans/` — new **dated** design/scoping docs (this file is one). The right home for a *map* of how to
  land the proof.
- `docs/worklog.md` — append-only narrative (append, never rewrite).

Anything in the sandbox may be speculative **provided it is tagged speculative** (see §3).

**Canonical / gated — touch ONLY through the front door:**

- `definitions/` · `argument/lemmas/` · `proofs/<id>/` · `report/`

Exploration **never edits these directly.** The only way an idea enters them is by *proposing* a registry shard
(`HANDOFF.md` **Recipe A**) with an honest `status` and `af: none`, which then goes through the linker
(`scripts/argument.py`), an af workspace (**Recipe B**), and a reviewer who is not you (Law L4). A scoped idea is
an input to that pipeline, not a shortcut around it.

## 2. Never touch these (hard "do not")

- **Any `proofs/<id>/` of a validated result.** Those 14 af workspaces are the crown jewels. Do not re-open,
  re-refine, or "improve" them. `af` **archives validated children on re-refine** — you can silently destroy
  consensus that took a prover + N fresh verifiers to establish.
- **Generated files** — `argument/INDEX.md`, `argument/DAG.md`, `definitions/INDEX.md`, `report/figures/dag.pdf`.
  They are regenerated by scripts (`argument.py --generate`, `gen-dag-figure.py`); hand-editing them is a
  guaranteed drift/merge mess and will be clobbered.
- **Locked definitions** (`consensus` / `original` / `cited`). Changing a def ripples through everything that
  references it (`CLAUDE.md` §9 stop condition, Law L2). A new exploratory term goes in a **new** `def-` shard,
  never by mutating an existing one. Drift on a shared term/alias = build failure.

## 3. Status honesty — this is *the* thing that protects the validated work

The repo's single guarded failure is a confident, plausible, **wrong** claim, and `check-provenance` treats a
**status OVERCLAIM** as the #1 failure. Therefore:

- An exploratory argument that "clearly works" is **`(open)`** or **`(conjecture)`** until it is an af-validated
  DAG node with **A+B consensus**. Plausibility is not proof.
- **Never** flip an `af:` field to `validated`, and never write a registry `status` stronger than the evidence.
  When scoping a route to close `op-layer1-gap`, the deliverable is *"here is a candidate path, here are the
  gaps and what each step would require,"* **not** *"Layer-1 is now essentially done."*
- **Read `docs/LEARNINGS.md` first.** It logs the claims this project already retracted because they "looked
  right" — the faithful-invariant `O(η)` overclaim, "Frobenius ⇒ order-unit," "square-hole is `O(η²)`," "use `η`
  in general." Those are the exact traps an optimistic explorer falls into. Re-read `CLAUDE.md` §3 (the
  hallucination-risk callouts) too.

## 4. Ground-truth discipline (the citation trap)

Exploration constantly reaches for "standard results." In this repo a fact only counts if it **byte-matches a
local `refs/` source** (`scripts/check-refs.py` enforces it; no paraphrase survives the gate).

- In sandbox notes, mark any from-memory fact **`[UNGROUNDED — needs refs/ source]`** so it can never be
  mistaken for established. You **may** dispatch researcher subagents to *find* an authoritative,
  byte-extractable source and *propose* adding it to `refs/` (manifest + sha256) — but the binding step is still
  a local byte-match, done through the gate.
- **Known landmines:**
  - `kaup-1984` and `chu-russo-1512.03347` are **SUSPECT** refs (recorded bytes match no authoritative origin —
    do **not** trust them).
  - `agent-B/notes/response-to-agent-a-v0.*` are **phantom** subagent extrapolations — do **not** cite your own
    predecessor's hallucinations as ground truth or as Agent A's positions. `agent-a-findings` is the single
    source of truth for A.
  - Paths like `/home/tobiasosborne/...` (and `/home/tobias/...`) in older notes are **stale**; refs are deduped
    into `refs/`.

## 5. Coordination on a shared repo (how work actually gets clobbered)

Beads is **non-functional in this clone** (empty DB, no dolt remote), so there is no task-locking — the **linker
(`scripts/argument.py`) is the only arbiter** of the canonical state. Practically:

- **Work on a feature branch**, not interleaved commits on `main`. Exploration churns; the validated trunk must
  stay clean. Rebase onto `main`; don't entangle your scratch commits with the validated record.
- **Run `sh scripts/check-all.sh` before every commit** and never bypass it (`core.hooksPath=/dev/null` is
  forbidden, Rule 5). There is no active pre-commit hook here — run it **by hand**.
- If a proposed shard **reds the linker** (cycle, drift, orphan, brittleness `>12` nodes / depth `>3`), that is a
  *stop-and-rethink*, not a *force-through*. A `>12`-node tree means *factor a sub-lemma*, not *relax the gate*.
- **Reviewer ≠ author** (Law L4, Rule 3): you scope; someone other than you (Agent A) validates before anything
  is tagged done. Don't be both the proposer and the judge of your own idea.

## 6. What good exploratory output looks like

The most valuable thing Agent B can hand back is **not** a half-proof sitting in the canonical layers — it is a
**map**. In `docs/plans/`, a dated doc that says:

> *"To land the full proof we need lemmas X, Y, Z. X reduces to [grounded `refs/` loci]. Y is the genuinely open
> hard part because … . Here is a proposed one-line `contract` for each, the `defs`/`deps` it would import, and
> what would red the linker. Here are the numerics that make me believe it / the counterexample that kills the
> naive version."*

That feeds straight into **Recipe A → Recipe B** without ever risking the validated work. A clean map of the
open frontier, with honest gaps and grounded reductions, is worth more than an overconfident "proof" that the
gates would (rightly) reject.
