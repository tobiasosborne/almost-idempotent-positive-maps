**Statement.** An algebra $A$ with a product $(a,b)\mapsto a\circ b$ (see [[def-jordan-product]]) is a
*Jordan algebra* if for all $a,b\in A$:
$$a\circ b = b\circ a \quad\text{(commutativity)}, \qquad a\circ(b\circ a^2)=(a\circ b)\circ a^2 \quad\text{(Jordan identity)},$$
where $a^2:=a\circ a$. By commutativity the Jordan identity is equivalent to the symmetric form
$((a\circ a)\circ b)\circ a=(a\circ a)\circ(b\circ a)$ (the form relaxed by JB4 of [[def-eps-jb-algebra]]).
A Jordan algebra need not be associative, but is *power-associative*: the subalgebra generated by one
element is associative, so $a^n$ is unambiguous ($a^{m+n}=a^m\circ a^n$).
