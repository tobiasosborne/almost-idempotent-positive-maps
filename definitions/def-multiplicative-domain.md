**Statement.** Every [[def-positive-unital-map]] $T$ satisfies the *Jordan–Schwarz* inequality
$T(a^*)\circ T(a)\le T(a^*\circ a)$ (Størmer). Its *multiplicative domain* is the equality set
$\mathrm{MD}(T)=\{a:\ T(a^*)\circ T(a)=T(a^*\circ a)\}$, which is a **J\*-algebra** (the Jordan analogue
of the fact that the multiplicative domain of a UCP map is a \*-algebra). For an idempotent UP map $E$,
$\mathrm{MD}(E)=\mathrm{Fix}(E)=\mathrm{Ran}(E)$. The *minimal sufficient J\*-algebra* of a family of UP
maps is the intersection of their fixed-point sets, generated by Neyman–Pearson tests.
