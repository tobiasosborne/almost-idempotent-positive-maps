**Statement.** A real [[def-jordan-algebra]] $A$ is *formally real* (or *Euclidean*) if
$\sum_{i=1}^n a_i^2 = 0$ in $A$ forces $a_1=\dots=a_n=0$. In finite dimensions a unital formally real
Jordan algebra is a [[def-jb-algebra]] in its order norm, and conversely every JB-algebra is formally
real — so in finite dimensions the two classes coincide.

**Notes / provenance.** HOS 3.1.7 and 3.3.8 (`refs/hos/joa-m.md`). Formal reality is exactly the
$\varepsilon=0$ content of JB3 (squares are positive) in [[def-eps-jb-algebra]]; its quantitative
relaxation is "approximate positivity of squares". The simple finite-dimensional formally real algebras
are classified by Jordan–von Neumann–Wigner (registry thm-jnw): $\mathbb R$, [[def-spin-factor|spin
factors]], $H_n(\mathbb R/\mathbb C/\mathbb H)$, and the exceptional $H_3(\mathbb O)$.
