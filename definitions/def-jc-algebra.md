**Statement.** A *JC-algebra* is a norm-closed [[def-jordan-algebra|Jordan]] subalgebra of
$B(\mathcal H)_{\mathrm{sa}}$ for some complex Hilbert space $\mathcal H$ (equivalently, a JB-algebra that
is *special* — isomorphic to one representable by self-adjoint operators under
[[def-jordan-product]]). Every JC-algebra is a [[def-jb-algebra]]. A JB-algebra that is **not** special is
*exceptional*; in finite dimensions the only exceptional one is the Albert algebra $H_3(\mathbb O)$. A
weak-closed JC-algebra is a *JW-algebra*.

**Notes / provenance.** HOS 3.1.2 (`refs/hos/joa-m.md`); exceptionality of $H_3(\mathbb O)$ HOS 2.8.5
(`joa-m.md:1856`, `:2306`). **Project-relevant:** ranges of positive unital projections (Effros–Størmer)
and the limit objects of the channel application are always special — $H_3(\mathbb O)$ provably never
arises. Reversibility (JW) is an extra property, not automatic (spin factors $V_4$, $V_{\ge6}$ are
irreversible). See [[def-spin-factor]], classification thm-jnw (registry).
